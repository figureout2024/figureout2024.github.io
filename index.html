A Survey of Reasoning Benchmarks for Large Language Models: Rethinking Benchmark Comparability

Chenyuan Zhang+, Simin Liu*, Hanjing Li+, Te Gao+, Yidi Wang+, Qiguang Chen*, Xiachong Feng‡, Xie Chen§, Libo Qin+ Min Zhang+
Harbin Institute of Technology+, Central South University*, The University of Hong Kong‡, Shanghai Jiao Tong University§

Abstract

This survey presents the first dedicated review of reasoning benchmarks for large language models from the perspective of benchmark design and interpretation. We argue that current benchmark results are often hard to compare because benchmarks differ not only in target capability, but also in test conditions and scoring criteria. To address this issue, we introduce a unified framework built around Object, Setting, and Evaluation. Object defines the reasoning capability under test. Setting specifies the conditions that shape model behavior. Evaluation determines how success is measured. We further use extended scenarios to show how benchmark conclusions change when object, setting, or evaluation change. Based on this analysis, we identify two major weaknesses in current practice, namely heterogeneous benchmark objects and weakly justified settings, and turn these findings into practical guidance for benchmark selection, construction, and reporting the future direction. We hope this work can provide a valuable foundation for more interpretable and implementable reasoning benchmarks.
